About Me

A quick glimpse at my work

My research examines the reproduction of social memory through performative acts of citizenship, such as monumental construction, institutional veneration, and commemoration, by individuals or civic bodies in the development and embellishment of public urban spaces in the Roman world. Informed by recent scholarly developments in the fields of citizenship, memory, and performance studies, my research looks at the archaeological remains of public urban spaces, from inscriptions and statues to buildings and features of spatial organization, holistically to understand how and why individuals and civic bodies constructed buildings, worshipped gods and rulers, and commemorated benefactions in the chosen manner.

My current project, entitled Ad perpetuitatis memoriam: Citizenship Performance and Social Memory in the Public Sphere at Lepcis Magna, presents an ‘urban biography’ of Lepcis Magna, drawing on archival and published material as well as recent developments in the fields of citizenship, memory, and performance studies to investigate how individuals and civic bodies contributed to the development and embellishment of Lepcis Magna, and how the commemoration of acts of euergetism, embedded within the city’s visual milieu, preserved social memory and perpetuated the performance of local and global citizenship in the city’s public spaces.

In my spare time, I am a hobbyist woodworker. I have made a number of projects, including end-grain cutting boards, drink coasters, and custom furniture and accessories.
